FOOD SUPPLY SAY IT LOUDER FOR THE PEOPLE IN THE BACK

The food landscape is absolutely, imo, the number one cause for widespread bad health from coronary disease to diabetes to cancer. The widespread availability of processed foods, many of which aren’t as strictly regulated as they should be?, makes it harder and harder to eat well. Cheaper to mass produce/better lobbying -> more subsidies -> cheaper to buy. Hence why being poor can do a number on your health not only psychologically but simply because your affordable food options are so! limited! Same with walkable cities. The American food landscape is pumped full of processed foods, our actual landscape is geared toward cars instead of people, and as a result our people are suffering. How do we acknowledge the needs of our people while moving toward a more healthful society?
